<p><p>Round countersunk screws come in various market types, primarily categorized as Cross (Phillips) and Suriwari (Minus). Cross (Phillips) screws feature a cross-shaped drive that allows for increased torque and better grip, making them ideal for various applications. In contrast, Suriwari (Minus) screws have a straight slot drive, often used in applications requiring simple installation with minimal risk of slipping. Each type offers distinct advantages, catering to different fastening needs across diverse industries.</p></p>
