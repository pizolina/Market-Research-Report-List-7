<p><p>The Human Immunoglobulin market comprises two primary administration routes: Intramuscular Injection (IM) and Intravenous Injection (IV). IM injections deliver immunoglobulins into the muscle, providing gradual absorption and a prolonged effect, making them suitable for outpatient settings. In contrast, IV injections deliver these therapies directly into the bloodstream for immediate therapeutic effects, often used in acute care or hospital environments. These modes cater to different patient needs, treatment timelines, and clinical scenarios, enhancing the versatility of immunoglobulin therapies.</p></p>
