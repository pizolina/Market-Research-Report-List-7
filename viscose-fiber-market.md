Viscose filament yarn is a long, continuous fiber used in weaving and knitting for producing smooth, shiny fabrics, ideal for luxury garments. In contrast, viscose staple fiber consists of shorter fibers, typically utilized for spinning into yarn or blended with other fibers, resulting in soft, absorbent textiles suitable for apparel and home furnishings. Both types cater to diverse applications in the textile industry.</p></p>
